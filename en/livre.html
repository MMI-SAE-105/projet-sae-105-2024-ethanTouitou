Artificial Intelligence, a Revolution

Prefaced by Olivier Tesquet, *Artificial Intelligence, a Revolution* is an illustrated book that offers a unique perspective on the rise of artificial intelligence through 120 editorial cartoons. The book explores the impacts of AI on society, ethics, and daily life while providing a critical and often humorous reflection on this increasingly omnipresent technology. This section delves into the book's key themes and analyses.

Book Overview:

*Artificial Intelligence, a Revolution* is a collection of 120 editorial cartoons that illustrate in a playful, critical, and accessible way the challenges of AI. This book offers a reflection on the future of artificial intelligence, its advancements, risks, and opportunities. The cartoons provide a unique and impactful perspective on how AI is infiltrating our lives, raising essential questions about its impact.

Analysis of Characters Related to Artificial Intelligence:

  • Officer K (Ryan Gosling): A replicant tasked with hunting down his own kind. K embodies the dilemma of artificial identity and the search for meaning in a world where he is not considered human.
  • Joi (Ana de Armas): An advanced domestic AI, representing a new type of relationship between man and machine. Joi illustrates the idea of an AI developing a form of emotional attachment, even if her actions are determined by algorithms.
  • Niander Wallace (Jared Leto): The creator of the new replicants, Wallace embodies the vision of a future where artificial intelligence is used for absolute control, without regard for ethics or morality.

The Visual Universe of the Film:

The film stands out for its futuristic and immersive aesthetic, where city architecture is dominated by towering skyscrapers and fluorescent neon lights. The desolate landscapes and omnipresent technology reinforce the idea of a dystopian world. The film's color palette, dominated by dark tones, cool blues, and fiery oranges, symbolizes both technological advancement and societal decay.
